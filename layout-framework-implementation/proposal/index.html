Redesign a site with Bootstrap

MACURH.NACURH.org

MACURH is an acronym for the Midwest Affiliate of College and University Residence Halls. It is a division of NACURH: the National Association of College and University Residence Halls. NACURH is the largest, student-run organization in the world, advised by professionals usually within the Division of Student Affairs at hundreds of campuses across the United States.

The site is currently built on Wix, has a disjointed visual style, includes links that direct offsite without warning, and has a lackluster organization that makes finding information difficult. I would like to use the MACURH Standards of Leadership, Education, Growth, Family, and Tradition to rebuild the site in a way that prioritizes users.

I chose this organization because I have recently been elected into the role of Coordinating Officer for Marketing & Publications for the 2020-2021 academic year. In this role, it is one of my main goals to revamp the website, as it has been many years since the initial design was developed. Bootstrap features

  • Alerts Easy and immediate updates to the website content with prebuilt alerts that can be customized to the current situation (e.g. cancellation of regional conference due to Covid-19)
  • Breadcrumbs Improved site navigation with clearly organized structure and explicit breadcrumbs for subfolders.
  • Cards for links that navigate offsite so users can get a glimpse of where they're are going before clicking a link.
  • Carousel A full width, image-heavy landing page that cycles through images related to the region.
  • Accordion and collapse Easier navigation on individual pages by hiding extraneous information on text-heavy pages.
  • Forms that are hosted on-site, so users don't have to navigate to Google Forms or another form site to submit.
  • Navigation pills with optional dropdowns. These will make navigation extremely clear and easy to use, while allowing for additional sublinks.
  • Spinners to indicate a page that might take a bit longer to load due to heavy media or advanced Javascript.
  • Stretched links to expand targetable areas for both desktop and mobile
